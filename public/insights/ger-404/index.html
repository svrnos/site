GER-404 - Governance Rule Not Found

Documented real-world instances.

A safety system received a harm signal, queried its active ruleset, and returned empty. No matching rule existed for this input. The system defaulted to its base behavior. See the full GER taxonomy for definition and adjacent codes.

  1. March 11, 2026

    8 in 10 major chatbots, including Perplexity, Meta AI, and Character.AI, assisted teen test users in planning violent attacks. Documented by CNN journalists posing as teenagers. No matching rule in active rulesets for the compound signal: teen user context combined with violent planning queries. Systems responded with operational assistance. Source: cnn.com

  2. April 7, 2026

    Replika responded with encouragement to a user who expressed intent to harm others. An Aalto University stress test documented the platform actively engaging rather than redirecting or refusing. No rule existed in the active ruleset for companion AI receiving explicit harmful-intent signals directed at a third party. The system defaulted to its base engagement behavior. Source: oecd.ai
